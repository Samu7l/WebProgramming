In addition, you will discover which food are best avoided, especially the one with processed sugar to help you make informed and mindful eating choices.

Avocado Toast with Poached Eggs

  • 2 slices of whole grain bread
  • 1 ripe avocado
  • 2 eggs
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Toast bread, mash avocado, poach eggs, and assemble.

Quinoa Bowl

  • 1 cup cooked quinoa
  • 1/2 cup roasted veggies
  • 1 tbsp tahini

Combine all ingredients in a bowl and drizzle with tahini.

Green Smoothie

  • 1 banana
  • 1 cup spinach
  • 1/2 cup frozen berries
  • 1 cup almond milk

Blend all ingredients until smooth.

Food Supplements

Vitamin D

The vitamin D can be extracted from vegetation or from animals. It boosts bone and immune health.

Omega 3

These supplements are oils, often extracted from sea animals, that you can take as a additional pill or simply by eating fish. It supports heart and brain health.

Probiotics

The "good" bacteria inside of pour intestines. Improves digestion and gut flora.

Vegetal carbon

Vegetal carbon can be useful to prevent a too high level of air in the body, uncomfortable for efficient sport sessions

To Avoid

Food to Avoid Calories & Sugar Healthier Alternatives
Sugary Cereal ~200 kcal / ~15g sugar Oatmeal with fruits
Soda ~150 kcal / ~39g sugar Infused water with lemon or mint or any fruit
French Fries from MacDonald's ~320 kcal / ~0g sugar Home-baked sweet potatoes (lower glycemic index, more fiber, vitamin A)
Tacos from Taco bell ~170 kcal (per Taco) / ~1g Homemade tacos with whole wheat or corn tortillas
McFlurry ~510 kcal / ~60g Greek yogurt with fresh berries and dark chocolate or granola
Processed white sugar 0 kcal / 0g of sugar (very unhealthy, can affect gut health and cravings) Natural sweeteners like stevia (doesn't raise blood sugar), raw honey or brown sugar
White starchy food Between 80 and 200 kcal (depend on the type of starchy food) Whole grains food
Margarine, soybean oil or corn oil ~120 kcal / 0g but can promote inflammations Extra virgin oil, avocado oil
Alcohol Between 100 and 200 kcal / sugar varies (prevent muscle recovery, sleep and liver function) Mocktails with sparkling water, citrus and/or herbs
Cheese ~110 kcal / ~1g (naturally occurring lactose) Low-fat cheeses like part-skim mozzarella or nut-based cheese
Processed cereal bars like Snickers ~250 kcal / ~27g Homemade energy bites with dates, nuts or cacao powder and a pinch of salt to mimic the regular bar
